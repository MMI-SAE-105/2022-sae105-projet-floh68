Tokyo Drift by Teriyaki Boyz

The history and background of Teriyaki Boyz

The Teriyaki Boyz are a Japanese-American hip-hop group from Los Angeles, California. The group was formed in 2006 by brothers James and John Yokoyama, and is well known to car fans who have watched all of the Fast & Furious saga, including lead actor Vin Diesel and others.

The Teriyaki Boyz released their first album in 2006, which included the singles "Gangsta Walk" and "Rice & Curry". In 2008, they released the single "Tokyo Drift", which was featured on the Fast and Furious soundtrack. The song peaked at number fifty-five on Billboard's Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart.

In 2009, they released their second album, entitled The Movement: Music Inspired by the Film Public Enemies. The album's first single "Nothin 'Like Us" peaked at number ninety-six on Billboard's Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart.

Link to Fast & Furious 3

The music of tokyo drift by Teriyaki boyz is closely linked with fast and furious 3. Indeed, the music is used in the film, which made this song by Teriyaki boyz known to a large audience all over the world, since the whole Fast and Furious saga was a success all over the world, and inspired many to become passionate about cars and big engines.
