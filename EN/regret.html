The disappearance of combustion engine vehicles

The famous petrol vehicles..

Gasoline-powered vehicles have many advantages, including the ability to transport people and goods quickly and easily. However, the invention of the internal combustion engine has led to some surprising results. In recent decades, the number of vehicles using this fuel has declined as car owners have chosen to purchase electric or hybrid vehicles. The decline in the use of gasoline-powered vehicles is a testament to humanity's environmental awareness and love for their cars. A number of factors have led to a decline in the use of petrol vehicles. Firstly, car ownership was mainly confined to urban areas at the time of invention. As a result, people lived further away from petrol stations than today. However, car owners now have more transport options; therefore, some choose not to drive if they live too far from a petrol station. Apart from this, people in rural areas often have to walk several blocks to a petrol station if they want to fill up. Although some people still choose to drive far from home, the average distance people drive today is much less than it was in the past, as many move to the city for example.

The law of 2035…

Indeed, recently the European Parliament voted in favour of banning the sale of new vehicles that are equipped with combustion engines (diesel or petrol) from 2035. The European Union has decided to stop the sale of its vehicles, which is also included in a set of texts presented by the European Commission in 2021.
